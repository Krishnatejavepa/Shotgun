## venture.md skeleton

- **Product:** <the service, productized or custom, and the niche it serves>
- **Customers / target users:** <client profile: industry, size, budget range, who signs>
- **Revenue model:** <retainer / project / productized packages, typical deal size>
- **Current numbers:** active clients, pipeline value, utilization %, average project margin, with as-of dates
- **Top 3 priorities (as of YYYY-MM-DD):**
  1.
  2.
  3.
- **Biggest risk right now:** <usually: one client being >40% of revenue, or feast-famine pipeline>

## Vault taxonomy that fits agencies

- `vault/clients/`: one subfolder per client: contracts, briefs, deliverables, meeting notes
- `vault/pipeline/`: leads, proposals sent, win/loss notes
- `vault/playbooks/`: how the service gets delivered (these become learned skills fast)
- `vault/finance/`: invoices, aging report, rate history
- `vault/marketing/`: case studies, portfolio pieces, outreach templates

## Numbers that matter

Pipeline value vs monthly target, revenue concentration (top client %), utilization, and invoice aging. The concentration number is the one founders avoid looking at; surface it monthly.

## Typical first open loops

- Proposals have no follow-up cadence, wins are leaking
- Client X's scope has crept 3 times unbilled, renegotiate
- No case study exists for the best project shipped this year
